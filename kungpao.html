Kung Pao Chicken

Kung pao chicken is a Chinese-style stir-fried dish featuring chicken cubes with dried chili peppers. It's a Sichuanese staple that's also popular globally, with variations adapted to local tastes. The Sichuan version is more complex, using Sichuan peppercorns and a large heap of dried chili peppers for extra spice.

This dish is made with chicken, chili peppers, and peanuts. It is flavored with soy sauce, scallions, and cornstarch for a glossy and smooth texture. Kung Pao Chicken is typically served with a side of white rice.

Ingredients

  1. 2 tablespoons white wine
  2. 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  3. 4 green onions, chopped
  4. 1 ounce hot chili paste
  5. 2 teaspoons brown sugar
  6. 1 teaspoon distilled white vinegar
  7. 2 tablespoons cornstarch, dissolved in water
  8. 4 ounces chopped peanuts
  9. 1 tablespoon chopped garlic
  10. 1 pound skinless, boneless chicken breast, cut into chunks

Instructions

Step Description
1 Gather all ingredients.
2 Combine water and cornstarch in a cup and set aside.
3 In a large bowl, mix 1 tablespoon wine, 1 tablespoon soy sauce, and 1 tablespoon cornstarch/water mixture for marinating the chicken.
4 In a medium bowl, mix the remaining wine, soy sauce, and cornstarch mixture. Add chili paste, brown sugar, vinegar, and peanuts. Stir well.
5 Heat the peanut mixture in a skillet over medium heat until aromatic.
6 Cook the marinated chicken in a separate skillet over medium-high heat until fully cooked.
7 Combine the cooked chicken with the peanut mixture and simmer until the sauce thickens.

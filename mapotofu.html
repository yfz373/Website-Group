    Mapo Tofu

    Mapo Tofu is a signature dish of Sichuan cuisine, renowned for its complex layers of flavor and fiery heat. The dish features soft, silky tofu submerged in a spicy, savory sauce that bursts with umami. The sauce is typically made with fermented black beans, chili paste, garlic, and Sichuan peppercorns, which add a unique numbing sensation to the spice.

    Traditionally, minced pork is added, giving the dish a hearty texture and deep flavor. The rich sauce coats each tofu piece perfectly, delivering a combination of heat, savory richness, and subtle sweetness. Mapo Tofu is a comforting, flavorful dish that embodies the bold, spicy nature of Sichuan cooking.

    Ingredients:

    1. Tofu (firm or soft, depending on preference)

    2. Ground pork or beef

    3. Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste)

    4. Douchi (fermented black beans)

    5. Garlic (minced)

    6. Ginger (minced)

    7. Green onions (chopped)

    8. Sichuan peppercorns

    9. Soy sauce

    10. Chinese cooking wine

    11. Chicken broth or water

    12. Cornstarch (for thickening)

    13. Sesame oil

    14. Sugar

    15. Chili oil

    Here are the steps of how to make Kung Pao Chicken.

    Step Description
    1. Prepare tofu: Cut the tofu into 1-inch cubes. Boil water in a pot and gently simmer the tofu for 2-3 minutes. This firms up the tofu and removes excess moisture. Drain and set aside.
    2. Prepare aromatics: Mince the garlic and ginger. Finely chop the white part of the green onions, reserving the green parts for garnish.
    3. Heat oil: In a large pan or wok, heat about 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat.
    4. Cook ground meat: Add the ground pork (or beef/vegetarian option) and stir-fry until it browns, breaking it up into small pieces. Add Shaoxing wine and cook for another 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
    5. Add aromatics: Add the minced garlic, ginger, and the white part of the green onions. Stir-fry for about 30 seconds until fragrant.
    6. Add doubanjiang and douchi: Stir in 1-2 tablespoons of doubanjiang (spicy fermented bean paste) and 1 tablespoon of douchi (fermented black beans). Stir-fry for 1-2 minutes to release the flavors.
    7. Season the sauce: Add 1 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 teaspoon sugar, chili flakes or chili oil (optional for extra heat), and Sichuan peppercorns (ground or whole). Stir everything together.
    8. Add stock: Pour in about 1 cup of chicken or vegetable stock. Bring the sauce to a boil and simmer for 2-3 minutes to let the flavors meld.
    9. Add tofu: Gently add the tofu cubes into the sauce, being careful not to break them. Let it simmer for another 5-7 minutes so the tofu absorbs the flavors.
    10. Thicken the sauce: Mix 1 tablespoon of cornstarch with 2 tablespoons of water to make a slurry. Stir the slurry into the pan and cook for another 1-2 minutes until the sauce thickens.
    11. Add sesame oil: Finish with a drizzle of sesame oil for extra fragrance.

Mapo Tofu

Mapo Tofu is a signature dish of Sichuan cuisine, renowned for its complex layers of flavor and fiery heat. The dish features soft, silky tofu submerged in a spicy, savory sauce that bursts with umami.

Traditionally, minced pork is added, giving the dish a hearty texture and deep flavor. The rich sauce coats each tofu piece perfectly, delivering a combination of heat, savory richness, and subtle sweetness.

Ingredients:

  1. Tofu (firm or soft)
  2. Ground pork or beef
  3. Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste)
  4. Douchi (fermented black beans)
  5. Garlic, minced
  6. Ginger, minced
  7. Green onions, chopped
  8. Sichuan peppercorns
  9. Soy sauce
  10. Chinese cooking wine
  11. Chicken broth or water
  12. Cornstarch (for thickening)
  13. Sesame oil
  14. Sugar
  15. Chili oil

Steps to Make Mapo Tofu

Step Description
1 Prepare tofu by cutting it into cubes, boiling for a few minutes, and setting aside.
2 Prepare aromatics by mincing garlic, ginger, and chopping green onions.
3 Heat oil in a wok and cook ground meat until browned.
4 Add doubanjiang, douchi, garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns. Stir-fry until fragrant.
5 Add soy sauce, stock, and tofu. Simmer for several minutes.
6 Thicken the sauce with cornstarch slurry and finish with sesame oil.

Chongqing Noodles

"Chongqing noodles, with its spicy and bold flavors, is a quintessential dish that represents the fiery spirit of Chongqing cuisine."

Chongqing noodles, also known as "Xiaomian," is a famous traditional snack from Chongqing, China. Known for its bold, spicy, and numbing flavors, it is a staple of Chongqing street food. The dish typically consists of alkaline noodles combined with a rich variety of seasonings, offering a unique and flavorful experience representative of the region's culinary style.

The key to Chongqing noodles lies in its rich combination of seasonings. With its spicy, numbing, and savory taste, paired with chewy noodles, it represents the bold and exciting flavors of Chongqing cuisine. Whether enjoyed as street food or made at home, Chongqing noodles have become beloved by food enthusiasts everywhere.

Ingredients:

  1. Fresh Wheat Noodles
  2. Chicken or Beef Broth
  3. Doubanjiang (fermented broad bean and chili paste)
  4. Chili Oil
  5. Sichuan Peppercorns
  6. Soy Sauce
  7. Chinese Black Vinegar
  8. Sesame Paste
  9. Garlic (Minced)
  10. Ginger (Minced)
  11. Scallions
  12. Crushed Peanuts
  13. Salt
  14. Sugar
  15. MSG

Steps to Make Chongqing Noodles:

Step Description
1 Gather all ingredients
2 Cook the noodles: Boil water in a large pot and cook the fresh wheat noodles according to the package instructions. Once cooked, drain the noodles and set them aside.
3 Prepare the aromatics: Mince garlic and ginger. Finely chop scallions, separating the white and green parts for cooking and garnish.
4 Heat the broth: In a small pot, bring chicken or beef broth to a simmer (around ½-1 cup per bowl). The broth is minimal in Chongqing noodles, serving more to coat the noodles rather than being a full soup.
5 Make the sauce: Mix together doubanjiang, chili oil, soy sauce, black vinegar, sesame paste, minced garlic, ginger, and Sichuan peppercorns in a bowl.
6 Pour broth: Ladle a small amount of hot broth (about ½ cup) over the noodles to lightly coat them.

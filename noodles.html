    Chongqing Noodles

    "Chongqing noodles, with its spicy and bold flavors, is a quintessential dish that represents the fiery spirit of Chongqing cuisine."

    Chongqing noodles, also known as "Xiaomian," is a famous traditional snack from Chongqing, China. Known for its bold, spicy, and numbing flavors, it is a staple of Chongqing street food. The dish typically consists of alkaline noodles combined with a rich variety of seasonings, offering a unique and flavorful experience representative of the region's culinary style.

    Ingredients

    • Alkaline noodles (or handmade noodles)
    • 1-2 tablespoons of chili oil
    • Ground Sichuan peppercorn to taste
    • 1 teaspoon minced garlic
    • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
    • 1 teaspoon vinegar
    • MSG or chicken bouillon to taste
    • Chopped green onions and cilantro
    • Hot broth or clear soup

    Instructions

    1. Boil the alkaline noodles in hot water for 3-5 minutes until fully cooked. Drain the noodles and set them aside.
    2. Prepare the seasoning base in a bowl: add chili oil, ground Sichuan peppercorn, minced garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, and a small amount of MSG or chicken bouillon. Adjust salt to taste.
    3. Place the cooked noodles into the seasoning bowl, then pour in hot broth or clear soup. Stir well so the noodles can absorb the flavors.
    4. Garnish with chopped green onions, cilantro, and optional toppings like crushed peanuts or pickled vegetables for extra texture.
    5. Mix everything thoroughly and enjoy your spicy and flavorful Chongqing noodles!

    The key to Chongqing noodles lies in its rich combination of seasonings. With its spicy, numbing, and savory taste, paired with chewy noodles, it represents the bold and exciting flavors of Chongqing cuisine. Whether enjoyed as street food or made at home, Chongqing noodles have become beloved by food enthusiasts everywhere.
